Game Design Workshop Chapter 9: Playtesting

Playtesting. From my perspective the author was correct in that playtesting is the most important part of the game design process. You don't if something works unitl you try it? Right! The best way to know if the designer(s) hit their goal is if the game is tested. And that doesn't mean just once. I found it interesting that Halo 3 had over 3000 hours of playtesting done. Thats over a third of a year and nearly 20TB used. Granted that Mircosoft can afford to do that, but the number is impressive to say the least. Though I didn't like the idea, however, from a scientific and social perspective on playtesting, using random strangers to playtest is a pretty good idea. Mainly because of the unbias. The opinions would be honest. However, the playtesters may not like the game type or games at all. Therefore resulting in scewed results. On the other hand the target audience though seemingly maybe good candidates to playtest, however they themselves maybe biased as well because the want to like the game and may convince themselves of liking it. Either way the designer in my opinion needs carefully choose a mixture of both audiences to playtest. However, I didn't see the author suggest the idea of having different testers test for different things. I felt that this was overlooked in the chapter and could be a useful suggestion. The author addressed note taking, asking questions, and data gathering. These a pretty import things because the playtesters don't always know what to test for. And as you saw on how many playtests that Microsoft does keeping logs and data from your playtests help the developers to access and implement their feedback.
